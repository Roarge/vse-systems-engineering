# [Concept Title]

A **concept** page explains an idea that spans one or more sources. Use
this type for framework descriptions, definitional explanations, and
cross-source syntheses.

## When to use this type

- You need to explain why an idea matters, not just what its syntax is.
- The content draws on more than one source or combines a source with
  plugin-specific application.
- A reader needs the idea to understand several other pages.

## What this page should contain

- A one-paragraph summary of the concept in plain language.
- The concept's definition, its key components, and where its boundaries
  are (what it is and what it is not).
- Cross-links to the `reference` pages that supply authoritative details.
- Cross-links to the `process` pages where the concept is applied.

## What this page should not contain

- Exhaustive syntax lists. Link out to a `reference` page instead.
- Workflow instructions. Link out to a `process` page instead.
- Project-specific decisions. Those belong in the consuming project's own
  documents, not in the plugin's wiki.

## Size guidance

Concept pages tend to run 100 to 250 lines. If the concept splits cleanly
into independent sub-concepts, split the page. A concept page that tries
to cover multiple ideas loses its explanatory force.
